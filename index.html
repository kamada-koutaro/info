Project

■ Online Group Discussion (2022-):

We study how to make online group discussions—via chat, video, and VR—work better for everyone. Our focus is group dynamics (e.g., social loafing, the tendency for individual effort to drop in groups). Through experiments and data analysis, we identify why this happens and how humans perceive this. Based on our findings, we provide design practical computer-mediated communication (CMC) tools that counter it.

Keywords: Remote Collaboration, Computer-Mediated Communication (CMC), Group Dynamics, Social Loafing

■ Human-AI Collaborative Decision-making (2023-):

AI tools can improve our decisions, but over-reliance on AI can also reduce decision quality. This project studies and builds human-AI interfaces that help people use AI well. For example, what kind of interfaces and their elements can foster appropriate trust in AI. The goal is simple: help people and AI make better decisions together.

Keywords: Human-AI Collaboration, AI-Based Decision-Making

■ VR System for Psychiatric Disorders (2023-):

We are developing a VR system for mental disorders. For example, we are aiming to measure social cognitive functions, which are strongly related to schizophrenia, in a way that is more realistic by using VR.

Keywords: VR, Social Cognition, Psychiatric Disorder
